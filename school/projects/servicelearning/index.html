This spring, to satisfy the ILS service-learning requirement, I had to do 25 hours of volunteer service with an organization/organizations of my choice. I decided to volunteer with three organizations: the Smithsonian, the National Archives, and the University of Maryland Arboretum.


My volunteer service for the Smithsonian and the National Archives involved transcribing and digitizing old documents to allow researchers to better access their contents. For the Arboretum, I helped weed flower beds and a median near the Smith School of Business.


More information about my volunteering can be found by accessing the links at the top of the page. Both of these very different experiences left me with a greater appreciation of the work that volunteers do and made me excited to volunteer again in the future.
